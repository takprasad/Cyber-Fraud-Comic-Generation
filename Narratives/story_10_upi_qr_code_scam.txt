Story 10: UPI QR Code Scam
Fraud Typology: Fake Buyer / QR Code Payment Reversal Fraud
Victim: Meera (homemaker selling items online)

Meera had been trying to sell some old household items through an online classifieds platform. She had posted a few items with photos and her phone number, hoping to make some extra money for the family. Within hours of posting, she received a message from someone claiming to be interested in buying all the items at once. The buyer sounded eager and genuine, quickly agreeing to her asking price without any negotiation. Meera was delighted - this seemed almost too easy. The buyer then said he would send the payment immediately and asked Meera to share a QR code to receive the money. Meera did not know much about UPI, but her daughter had set it up on her phone, so she opened the app and shared the QR code as asked. The buyer said the payment had been sent and told her to scan a new QR code he would send to "confirm receipt" on her end. Trusting his words, Meera scanned the code he sent. Instead of receiving money, her phone showed a payment request - but she did not notice the difference and entered her UPI PIN. Within seconds, Rs.15,000 was deducted from her account. Confused, she called the buyer but the number was already switched off. Her daughter arrived home shortly after and explained what had happened - scanning a QR code sent by someone else means you are paying them, not receiving money from them. Meera had been deceived by a well-known QR code reversal scam that targets first-time UPI users.

Key Awareness Message: Scanning a QR code always means you are making a payment, never receiving one. Never scan a QR code sent by a buyer. To receive money, simply share your UPI ID or phone number.
