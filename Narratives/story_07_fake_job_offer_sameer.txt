Story 07: Fake Job Offer - Video Interview Scam
Fraud Typology: Work-From-Home Fraud / Fake HR / Training Fee Scam
Victim: Sameer (job seeker)
Fraudster: Neha (posing as HR manager)

Sameer had been searching endlessly for a stable work-from-home job. Every day he scrolled through job portals and email alerts, hoping something genuine would appear. One morning, he finally received an email that lifted his spirits - it claimed he had been shortlisted for a well-paying remote role. Excited and relieved, Sameer opened the email and quickly followed the instructions. Soon after, he received a video-call invite from a woman named Neha, who introduced herself as the HR manager. She spoke confidently, used corporate language, and maintained a professional tone throughout the interview. Her convincing manner made Sameer believe the opportunity was real. During the call, Neha explained the job role, benefits, and flexible working hours. Everything sounded perfect. Just when Sameer felt everything was falling into place, she mentioned a small condition - he needed to pay a "refundable training fee" before joining. Believing this was a normal onboarding requirement, Sameer made the payment through the link she shared. But soon after the transfer, things changed. Neha stopped responding. The email thread went silent. When Sameer tried to contact the number she had used earlier, he discovered he had been blocked. Reality hit him hard - he had been scammed. The entire setup, the professional tone, the fake interview - all of it was a trap. Sameer sat in shock, realizing that real HR departments never ask candidates to pay training fees, processing fees, or OTPs.

Key Awareness Message: Legitimate employers never ask candidates to pay any fees before joining. If a job offer demands upfront payment, it is a scam. Report to cybercrime helpline 1930.
