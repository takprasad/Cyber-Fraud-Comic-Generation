Story 03: Bank Details Extraction Scam
Fraud Typology: Social Engineering / Accidental Transfer Pretext
Victim: Shankar (farmer)
Fraudster: Rohit (young stranger)

Shankar was a hardworking farmer who lived a quiet life. Every morning he went to his fields, tending to the soil and crops with patience that came from years of experience. One warm afternoon, as he was digging the earth and wiping the sweat from his forehead, he noticed a young boy approaching on a bright red bicycle. The youngster introduced himself as Rohit. He looked worried and told Shankar that he had made a mistake - he claimed he had accidentally transferred Rs.2,000 to Shankar's account. Shankar did not understand much about such digital payments, but he stopped working and listened politely. Rohit spoke confidently and in a hurry, which made Shankar feel even more confused. When Shankar told him he didn't use UPI, Rohit immediately asked for his bank details, saying he needed them to "reverse the payment." Shankar, who had never faced such situations and believed people easily, thought the boy must really be in trouble. Wanting to help, he took Rohit home, pulled out his old passbook, and read out all his account details carefully. Rohit thanked him and left quickly. Shankar returned to his field, thinking he had helped someone in need. But the next day, when Shankar visited the bank to update his passbook, his hands began to tremble. A huge amount - Rs.50,000 - had been withdrawn from his account. He had never seen such a big loss before. His heart sank. Only then did he realize that Rohit had used the bank details to cheat him. Shankar felt devastated. A boy he trusted for just a few minutes had taken away the hard-earned savings of many months.

Key Awareness Message: Never share your bank account number, IFSC code, or passbook details with strangers. No legitimate reversal process requires your full bank details.
