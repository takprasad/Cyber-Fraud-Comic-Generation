Story 01: Online Gaming Scam
Fraud Typology: Fake In-Game Purchase Portal / UPI Fraud
Victim: Aarav (young boy)

Aarav had been enjoying his evening, completely lost in the world of mobile gaming. As he played, a bright pop-up suddenly appeared on his screen: "1000 Diamonds for Rs.199 - Official Offer." It looked so real, so tempting, that he didn't even hesitate. He tapped on it instantly, excited about getting a huge reward for such a small amount. The link took him to a website that looked exactly like the real game store - same colors, same buttons, same layout. Aarav didn't think for a second that anything was wrong. He just wanted those diamonds quickly. Without much thought, he entered his parents' UPI PIN, believing it was a simple in-game purchase. The moment he paid, the site flashed "Payment Failed." But the money was gone. Aarav assumed it was a glitch and tried again. Every time he found another similar offer, he repeated the same steps, hoping it would work just once. Instead, he kept losing more and more money, unaware of what was actually happening. Hours later, his parents noticed several unknown UPI transactions on their phones. Worried and confused, they confronted Aarav. Nervously, he explained everything - how the offers appeared, how the payments failed, and how he thought he was just buying in-game diamonds. When they checked the website he had visited, the truth hit them hard. It was a fake portal, cleverly designed to look like the real game store, meant only to steal UPI details from young gamers. As they dug deeper, they discovered that it was part of a major online gaming scam targeting kids with fake diamond offers. Aarav had fallen right into the trap, just like many others his age - simply because the scam looked exactly like the game he loved.

Key Awareness Message: Never enter UPI PIN or payment details on third-party gaming websites. Always purchase in-game items only through the official app store.
