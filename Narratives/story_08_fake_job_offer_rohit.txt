Story 08: Fake Job Offer - Online Advertisement Scam
Fraud Typology: Fake Part-Time Job Ad / Registration Fee Fraud
Victim: Rohit (college student)

Rohit had been searching for a simple part-time job to support his studies. Between classes and assignments, he often browsed job listings on his phone, hoping to find something flexible and genuine. One afternoon, a bright, eye-catching advertisement appeared on his screen - it promised Rs.5,000 per day for easy online work. The offer felt almost too good to be true, but Rohit's need for extra income made him curious. He clicked on the ad and was soon contacted by someone claiming to be a recruiter. The messages looked professional, filled with screenshots of "successful payments" and satisfied workers. Everything was crafted to look real. Feeling reassured, Rohit continued chatting with the recruiter. But soon, the tone shifted. The recruiter explained that before starting, Rohit needed to pay small amounts labeled as registration fees, training charges, and security deposits. Wanting the job and afraid to miss out, Rohit paid the amount without overthinking. For a moment, he felt hopeful - maybe this would finally be the opportunity he needed. But just minutes after the payment went through, everything changed. The recruiter stopped responding. Rohit tried calling and messaging, but the number had been blocked. The entire page disappeared as if it had never existed. A sinking feeling hit him. He sat quietly with his phone in his hand, realizing he had been tricked by a fake job offer - one of the many scams targeting students every day.

Key Awareness Message: No genuine part-time job requires registration fees, training charges, or security deposits. If an online job demands payment before you start working, it is a scam.
