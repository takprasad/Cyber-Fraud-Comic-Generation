Story 04: Loan App Blackmail / Digital Extortion
Fraud Typology: Predatory Loan App / Morphed Image Blackmail
Victim: Priya (young woman)

Priya had just returned home after a long and tiring day. Wanting to relax for a moment, she sat on her bed and casually scrolled through her phone, unaware that a hidden danger was about to enter her life. As she browsed, an eye-catching ad suddenly popped up, promising instant money with no documents required. It seemed simple, quick, and harmless. Curiosity mixed with a bit of financial stress pushed her to click on it. The app looked professional and easy to use, so Priya went ahead and installed it. It asked for some basic details and a selfie for verification. Trusting the polished interface, she uploaded everything without overthinking. Within minutes, money was deposited into her account. Priya was surprised but relieved, thinking the app was genuine. But soon after, things took a frightening turn. The app began demanding an outrageous repayment amount - far higher than what she had received. Before she could make sense of it, a threatening caller contacted her. His tone was harsh, pressuring her to pay immediately. Priya tried explaining, but he only grew more aggressive. Then the situation worsened - the scammers had taken her selfie and edited it into inappropriate images. They began sending these fake, doctored photos to her contacts, accusing her of things she never did. Priya's phone buzzed nonstop as friends and relatives started receiving abusive messages through her name. Feeling terrified and helpless, Priya watched her reputation crumble while the scammers continued to intimidate her. Somewhere far away, the man behind the scam sat comfortably in front of his computer, smiling with satisfaction as he uploaded more threats and continued the harassment.

Key Awareness Message: Never install unverified loan apps. Never upload your selfie or personal documents to unknown apps. If blackmailed, report immediately to cybercrime helpline 1930.
