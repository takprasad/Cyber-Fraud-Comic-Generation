Story 06: Fake Sponsorship Scam
Fraud Typology: Social Media Advance Fee Fraud / Fake Sponsor
Victims: Riya and Akash (college students)

Riya and Akash were spending the afternoon in their college library, finalizing plans for their upcoming TechFest. While Akash worked on budgeting, Riya scrolled through Instagram, checking updates and replies related to event promotions. Suddenly, a new message popped up in her inbox - an Instagram page claiming to be a well-known sponsor. The message sounded exciting: they wanted to sponsor the TechFest and were offering an attractive Rs.50,000 advance. Curious and hopeful, Riya quickly showed the message to Akash. Both of them felt unsure, but the offer looked promising enough to explore. The so-called sponsor continued messaging, insisting that the advance payment would be processed soon. However, before releasing the amount, they asked Riya to pay a small Rs.499 "processing fee." The amount seemed minor compared to the promised sponsorship, and despite her hesitation, Riya went ahead and paid it. But the demands didn't stop there. Soon the page began asking for additional payments, each time with a new excuse - verification charges, file handling fees, and so on. Riya started feeling uneasy as the messages grew pushier and more frequent. Before she could gather her thoughts or ask Akash for help, the scammer suddenly blocked her. The profile disappeared, along with her money, leaving Riya stunned and frustrated. It didn't take long for her to realize what had happened - she had fallen victim to a fake sponsorship scam. The truth became painfully clear: legitimate sponsors never ask for processing fees.

Key Awareness Message: Legitimate sponsors never ask for upfront fees or processing charges. Always verify sponsor identity through official channels before making any payment.
