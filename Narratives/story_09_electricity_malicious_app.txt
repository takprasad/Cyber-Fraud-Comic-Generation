Story 09: Electricity Department Malicious App Scam
Fraud Typology: Utility Impersonation / Remote Access Malware
Victim: Arvind (working professional)

Arvind began his afternoon like any other, checking his monthly electricity bill on his mobile phone. Everything seemed normal until a sudden SMS flashed on his screen - a warning that his electricity connection would be cut off immediately due to "pending verification." The message looked urgent and official, instantly making him anxious. Before he could think, his phone rang. The caller introduced himself confidently as an officer from the electricity department. Speaking in a firm, authoritative tone, the man claimed that Arvind's power would be disconnected within minutes unless he completed a quick "technical update." Worried about losing electricity, Arvind listened closely. The caller then instructed him to download a specific app on his phone, assuring him it was an official update required to fix his account. Trusting the urgency in the caller's voice, Arvind went ahead and installed the app. Unaware of the danger, he granted all permissions the app requested - camera, SMS, notifications, and even full device access. The caller continued sounding helpful and cooperative, convincing Arvind that everything was under control. But moments after the installation, Arvind's phone lit up with repeated transaction alerts. One after another, money started disappearing from his bank account. Confused and terrified, Arvind realized he had unknowingly installed a malicious app designed to steal his financial information. By the time he tried to reach the caller again, the number was unreachable.

Key Awareness Message: Electricity departments never ask you to download apps or share OTPs over the phone. Never install apps from unknown links. Delete suspicious apps immediately and report to cybercrime helpline 1930.
