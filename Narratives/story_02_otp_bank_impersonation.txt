Story 02: OTP Bank Impersonation Fraud
Fraud Typology: Vishing / Fake Bank Officer / OTP Theft
Victim: Arun (salaried professional)
Fraudster: Kavya (posing as bank fraud officer)

Arun was a regular office-goer who liked to keep his finances organized. One evening, he sat at his desk at home, sorting through his monthly bills and updating his accounts. Everything seemed normal until his phone suddenly rang. On the screen, it showed an unknown number. When he answered, a serious voice introduced herself as Kavya, supposedly calling from his bank's fraud department. She sounded professional and confident. Arun felt a slight worry as she told him there had been a suspicious transaction attempted from his account. Before he could think clearly, panic began to rise inside him. The thought of someone stealing his hard-earned money scared him. Kavya continued speaking calmly, guiding him step by step. She said the bank needed to "verify" that Arun was the real account holder. For that, he had to share the OTP he would soon receive on his phone. Arun hesitated, but Kavya's tone was reassuring. She kept insisting that without the OTP, the bank wouldn't be able to stop the fraud. Believing she was helping him protect his money, Arun finally shared the OTP with her. Within minutes, new messages started popping up on his phone - but they were not confirmations of fraud being stopped. Instead, they were debit alerts. Money was leaving his account in multiple transactions. Arun's heart sank as he realized something was terribly wrong. By the time he understood what had happened, Kavya had already ended the call. Sitting in front of her computer somewhere far away, she smiled - another innocent victim had just been successfully scammed.

Key Awareness Message: Banks never ask for OTP over the phone. Never share your OTP with anyone, including someone claiming to be from your bank.
